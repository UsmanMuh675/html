🍕 Classic Margherita Pizza

A timeless Italian masterpiece — fresh basil, creamy mozzarella, and tangy tomato sauce on a crispy yet chewy crust. This vegetarian pizza captures the essence of Naples in every bite. Perfect for a family dinner or weekend cooking project.

📋 Recipe Details

  • ⏲️ Prep Time: 20 minutes (plus 1 hour dough resting)
  • 🔥 Cook Time: 12–15 minutes
  • 🍽️ Servings: 4 slices (2-3 people as main)
  • ⭐ Difficulty: Easy to Medium — perfect for home bakers

🛒 Ingredients

For the dough:

  • 2 ½ cups (300g) all-purpose flour (or tipo 00 flour)
  • 1 teaspoon instant yeast
  • 1 teaspoon salt
  • 1 teaspoon sugar (optional, for browning)
  • 1 cup (240ml) warm water (about 110°F / 45°C)
  • 1 tablespoon olive oil

For the topping:

  • ½ cup (130g) tomato sauce (San Marzano preferred)
  • 8 oz (225g) fresh mozzarella, sliced or torn
  • Fresh basil leaves (handful)
  • 2 tablespoons extra virgin olive oil
  • Optional: Parmesan cheese and red pepper flakes

Note: Fresh ingredients make all the difference — use ripe tomatoes if making homemade sauce.

👨‍🍳 Instructions

  1. Prepare the dough: In a large bowl, mix flour, yeast, salt, and sugar. Add warm water and olive oil, stirring until a shaggy dough forms. Knead on a floured surface for 8–10 minutes until smooth and elastic.
  2. First rise: Place dough in an oiled bowl, cover with a damp cloth, and let rise in a warm place until doubled in size (about 1 hour).
  3. Shape the pizza: Punch down the dough. On a floured surface, stretch or roll it into a 12-inch (30cm) circle. Transfer to a parchment-lined baking sheet or pizza peel.
  4. Add toppings: Spread tomato sauce evenly leaving a ½-inch border. Arrange fresh mozzarella pieces and scatter a few basil leaves. Drizzle with extra virgin olive oil.
  5. Bake to perfection: Preheat oven to 475°F (245°C) with a pizza stone or baking sheet inside. Bake the pizza for 12–15 minutes until crust is golden and cheese is bubbly with brown spots.
  6. Finish and serve: Remove from oven, top with remaining fresh basil leaves, a pinch of salt, and an extra drizzle of olive oil. Slice and serve hot!

💡 Pro tip: For an extra crispy crust, bake the pizza on the lowest oven rack for the final 2 minutes.

📌 Tips & Notes

  • Make ahead: The dough can be refrigerated for up to 3 days. Bring to room temperature before shaping.
  • No pizza stone? Use an inverted baking sheet preheated in the oven — works great!
  • Vegetarian & nut-free: This recipe is naturally vegetarian; skip optional parmesan for a dairy-free version (use vegan cheese).
  • Difficulty tip: If you’re short on time, store-bought pizza dough is an acceptable shortcut.
